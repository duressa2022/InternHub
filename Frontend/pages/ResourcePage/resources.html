Cover Letter Guide

1. Header & Greeting

Include your contact info and address the hiring manager by name if possible.

2. Opening Paragraph

Mention the position you're applying for and your enthusiasm for the role.

3. Body Paragraphs

Highlight 2-3 relevant experiences or skills that match the job requirements.

4. Closing Paragraph

Express your interest in an interview and thank the reader.

Resume Writing Tips

  • Keep it to one page (for students and recent grads)
  • Use strong action verbs (developed, led, implemented)
  • Quantify achievements when possible
  • Tailor your resume for each application
  • Use a clean, professional format
  • Proofread multiple times for errors

Interview Preparation Resources

Common Interview Questions

"Tell me about yourself"

Structure your answer with: Present (current role/studies), Past (relevant experience), Future (goals and how this role fits).

"Why do you want this internship?"

Connect your skills/interests to the company's work and culture.

"What's your greatest strength?"

Pick one relevant to the role and provide a specific example.

"Describe a challenge you faced"

Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
